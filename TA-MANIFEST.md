## Test Automation Manifest

#### Principle 1 - Transparency over Comfort

***Test automation must be highly visible to generate added value**. We enable transparency, even if this means that we have to expose mistakes in our own work.*

**Practical meaning:** A healthy automation practice makes failures, gaps, flaky tests, maintenance problems, and weak coverage visible. The message is that hiding issues may feel easier in the short term, but visibility gives the team a better basis for decisions.

#### Principle 2 - Collaboration over Independence

***It is better to collaborate and connect with other stakeholders and organizations than to solve problems on your own.***

**Practical meaning:** Automation should not be isolated inside QA only. It works better when testers, developers, DevOps, product people, and other stakeholders share responsibility and context.

#### Principle 3 - Quality over Quantity

***Reliable results that drive further work are more important than a high number of automated test cases.***

**Practical meaning:** The number of automated tests is not the main success metric. More value comes from stable, trustworthy, maintainable tests that help the team move forward.

#### Principle 4 - Flexibility over Continuity

***Rather than rigid structures, we prefer a flexible approach that can withstand future challenges.***

**Practical meaning:** Automation frameworks and processes should be adaptable.  
The message here is to avoid overly rigid designs that become hard to update when the product, architecture, or priorities change.
